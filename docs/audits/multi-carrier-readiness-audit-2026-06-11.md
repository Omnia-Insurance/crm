# Multi-Carrier Readiness Re-Audit — 2026-06-11

> **Status update (2026-06-12):** all 32 endorsed proposals below (18 small, 12 medium,
> 2 large) have been implemented, in six waves of work on this branch. This document is
> a point-in-time audit and is kept unedited; the gaps, dead knobs, and "walls" it
> describes no longer reflect the code. For the current state — including the carrier
> picker, the generic validated seed command, the self-describing status-engine
> registry, identifier roles, and the remaining honest walls — see
> [docs/reconciliation/carrier-onboarding.md](../reconciliation/carrier-onboarding.md)
> and [docs/reconciliation/status-engine-authoring.md](../reconciliation/status-engine-authoring.md).

Scope: the reconciliation flow as of `feature/reconciliation-remediation` (post-remediation,
commit ba267dbf0e). Question: how close is the carrierConfig vision — onboard UHO/BCBS by
reusing Ambetter's logic, and tune behavior on the fly — and what still stands in the way?

Method: 6 lens agents (config surface, status engines, matching, parse/transform, domain/UI,
operability), every proposal checked by an independent feasibility judge (42 agents total).
All endorsed proposals are additive: defaults preserve Ambetter behavior bit-for-bit.

## Verdict

The parse layer is genuinely config-driven — the two-carrier e2e proves a second ACA carrier
with different headers, date formats, booleans, and policy pattern is pure config. But the
**match/diff/status layers are still code**, several validated knobs are **dead** (the config
object accepts and stores them; nothing reads them), a new carrier **silently inherits
Ambetter defaults** (the 2025-07-09 startDate, the Ambetter status engine via fallback), and
the run wizard **cannot even select a second carrier**. UHO-class carriers (policy-number
identifiers, ACA-like billing) are one status engine + a handful of knobs away. BCBS-class
carriers (member-ID-centric, subscriber/group numbers, member-level rows) additionally hit
the hardcoded identity model, dedup policy, and tier internals.


## Lens summaries

### Config-surface completeness

The zod boundary (types/carrier-config.ts) is genuinely solid: every stored field is validated, partial-merged over defaults, and the two-carrier e2e proves a second ACA carrier with different headers/dates/booleans/policy-pattern is pure config. But the config surface is honest only at the parse layer; at match/diff/status layers most behavior-determining decisions are still code: the status-engine registry has exactly one entry, every tier's confidence constants except tier 7 are literals, dedup and diff-suppression policy (including the agent-of-record skip the business goal explicitly cares about) are hardcoded, and the matching role registry cannot express a member-ID-centric identifier living on a non-policyNumber CRM field. Worse for on-the-fly tuning, three knobs the boundary happily validates (enableMissingFromBob, discoveryNameThreshold/discoveryAutoThreshold, paymentErrorAgeDays) are connected to nothing, enabledTiers accepts any string so a typo silently disables a tier, and the review UI's batch-apply scope ignores the carrier's autoMatchThreshold. The single most dangerous new-carrier default is DEFAULT_MATCHING_CONFIG.startDate = '2025-07-09' (the Ambetter onboarding date), silently inherited by any carrier config that omits the key. Net: UHO is plausibly config-plus-one-status-engine; BCBS (member-level rows, shared subscriber IDs) additionally hits the hardcoded dedup/tier internals. All proposals below are additive with defaults preserving Ambetter behavior bit-for-bit.

### Status-engine extension story

The registry pattern is real and the seams are validated — engineId dispatch with fail-fast at PARSE and MATCH, role-based fieldMapping with header normalization, and a tunable placedThresholdDays — and the Oscar e2e (pipeline-real-data.spec.ts:1159-1314) proves an ACA-like, paid-through-centric carrier is a pure-config exercise against the single 'ambetter-bob-v1' engine. But the extension story stops at "ACA-like": deriveAmbetterStatus mixes three layers — generic mechanics (term-date-past cancel, future-effective approved, previous-version cancel, full-month/30-day placement: status.ts:270-285, 315-322, 188-203, 343-345) that are reusable, Ambetter billing semantics (eligibleForCommission=No as THE cancel signal at 253-268; payment-error = paid-through < current-month-end with no grace concept at 177-186, 325-332) that are not, and a frozen 4-role input contract (status.ts:48-53) typed only date|boolean (28-35) that cannot carry premium-paid amounts, lapse flags, or an explicit status column at all. A BCBS-style BOB with an explicit status column needs a pass-through/mapping engine, and the registry cannot support one today — the function slot exists, but StatusInput physically cannot deliver a text status value, and there is no per-engine params channel (unknown statusConfig keys are zod-stripped, carrier-config.ts:101, 128-135). Verdict on declarative-vs-TS: a general rule DSL is not justified at 2-3 carriers; the right path is (1) promote the registry entry to a StatusEngine interface declaring roleTypes/requiredRoles/paramsSchema, (2) ship exactly one declarative engine — an explicit-status mapping engine whose statusMap lives in engineParams — which covers the pass-through class on the fly, and (3) keep novel billing semantics (UHO premium-amount or grace-window logic) as small TS engines composed from the currently-private generic helpers. New-engine registration remains a deploy under this plan, but per-carrier behavior within an engine becomes workspace-record tunable.

### Matching genericity

Post-remediation, the matching engine is genuinely config-fed at a validated boundary: per-carrier thresholds, tier toggles, start-date cutoff, policy-number gate regex, overrides, and column-mapping-driven roles all flow from the carrierConfig workspace record, and the Oscar e2e proves a second policy-number-centric carrier works config-only. However, the engine's identity model is policy-number-shaped to its core: MatchInput has exactly one identifier role (policyNumber), so member-ID-centric carriers (UHO, BCBS) can only onboard by aliasing their member/subscriber ID onto the policyNumber role — losing subscriber-vs-member-vs-group distinctions entirely. Normalization is fixed at trim+uppercase with no per-carrier canonicalization (leading zeros, alpha prefixes, separators), tier ORDER and per-tier confidences (98/95/90/85/70/60) are constants, the Tier-6 weights (+30 agent bonus, x40 date, x20 name, +10 DOB) and date-proximity bands are hardcoded and ignore dateToleranceDays, and three knobs the zod boundary happily accepts (enableMissingFromBob, discoveryNameThreshold, discoveryAutoThreshold) drive nothing in the server pipeline — their only implementation lives in the legacy twenty-apps script with hardcoded Ambetter logic. Verdict: a UHO/BCBS file whose primary key resembles a single unique policy-number-like string can ship with config alone; a carrier needing multiple identifier signals, identifier canonicalization, or re-weighted tiers needs code. All proposed extension points can default to current Ambetter behavior (logicChange: false throughout).

### Parse/transform genericity

The parse/transform layer is genuinely config-driven for the easy 70%: per-carrier transformRules (slash-date order, year pivot, boolean tokens, currency stripping), role-based statusFieldMapping with header normalization, computed fields with role-name input resolution, per-run sheetName, and a columnMapping capture flow that works even for a never-seeded carrier — all validated at a single zod boundary and tunable without deploy. But the realities UHO/BCBS will bring are mostly NOT covered: header rows below row 1 (dialog lets you pick one, server re-parses from row 1), written/dash date formats (closed two-value enum), footer/total rows, percentage semantics, and multi-file periods all require pre-massaging files by hand. Two operability traps undermine 'onboard by config': the wizard hardcodes the first carrierConfig record so a second carrier cannot even be selected, and a never-seeded carrier silently falls back to the Ambetter engine with an empty role mapping that passes validation and blanket-derives statuses. The computed-field vocabulary (maxDate/minDate/coalesce) is too small for member-ID-centric carriers (no concat, no arithmetic, no value-map/conditional) and a typo'd method is silently skipped rather than fail-fast. All fixes can be additive extension points; none require changing Ambetter behavior.

### Domain model & UI assumptions

Post-remediation, the server pipeline is genuinely config-driven for carriers that share Ambetter's ACA shape: column mapping, status-role mapping, transform vocabulary, matching knobs, policy-number pattern, and product mapping all flow through the validated parseCarrierPipelineConfig boundary, matching is carrier-scoped, and the Oscar e2e proves a config-only second carrier — but only because Oscar deliberately reuses the one registered status engine. The hard ACA-individual assumptions live in three places: a single 'ambetter-bob-v1' entry in the status-engine registry with a closed 6-value OmniaStatus union and hardcoded terminal/active status sets (so BCBS grace-period or text-status semantics need code + deploy, and workspace-added SELECT options silently misclassify in matching narrowing, reinstatement flagging, and diff suppression); a fixed policy-number-centric identifier model in MatchInput/MATCHING_ROLE_BY_CRM_FIELD (member-ID carriers like UHO must masquerade as policyNumber); and universal diff suppressions (agent.* never synced, all currency diffs hidden, ACA rollover rules) that are Omnia business rules baked as cross-carrier law. The operator workflow is the first thing that breaks: the import wizard hardcodes records[0] with no carrier picker, and there is no UI or generic command to create a carrierConfig — today an engineer either clones the Ambetter seed command or an admin hand-authors RAW_JSON that is validated only when the run fails at PARSE. The UnmatchedView create-policy flow additionally re-implements the Ambetter status engine client-side with hardcoded thresholds and the literal 'eligible_for_commission' key, so unmatched UHO/BCBS rows would get Ambetter-derived statuses. On-the-fly tunability today covers thresholds and vocabulary, not semantics.

### On-the-fly tunability & operations

The remediated boundary makes match-time behavior genuinely live-tunable: edit matchingConfig/statusConfig/policyNumberPattern/productMapping on the carrierConfig record and the next run (including a REVIEW->MATCHING re-run) picks them up, with malformed JSON failing fast with key-level messages routed to errorMessage. But the operator loop is half-built: there is no way to validate an edit before burning a real run, misspelled knobs and unknown computed-field methods are silently ignored (zod strips unknown keys; COMPUTATIONS lookup no-ops), and every diagnostic warning (onWarning, cell-level parse errors, legacy fallbacks) goes only to worker logs while the review UI renders neither stats nor errorMessage. Parse-time knobs (transformRules, fieldConfig, columnMapping) cannot be re-applied to an existing run because VALID_TRANSITIONS has no path back to PARSING from REVIEW/COMPLETED, and since only columnMapping snapshots per run, an edit landing between the auto-chained parse and match reads produces mixed-config results with no per-run config fingerprint to detect it. Operationally, config editing is admin-only raw-JSON cell editing (300x400px popup) with no versioning or rollback, renaming a carrierConfig silently orphans learned rules and overrides (carrierName is the join key and is baked into signature hashes), and the run wizard hardcodes records[0], so a second carrier cannot even be selected in the UI. The highest-leverage additive work is a validateCarrierConfig resolver with header-resolution preview against the last parsed file, unknown-key warnings at the boundary, persisted run warnings plus a config hash in the stats JSON, and a carrier picker in the wizard.

## Gaps

### BLOCKS ANY NEW CARRIER (6)

#### New carriers silently inherit Ambetter's onboarding-date cutoff

*Lens: config-surface — `packages/twenty-server/src/modules/reconciliation/engines/matching.ts:308-315; types/carrier-config.ts:248`*

DEFAULT_MATCHING_CONFIG.startDate = '2025-07-09' is an Omnia business-history constant (the comment itself says 'not a sensible cutoff for any other carrier'), but the defaults merge in parseCarrierPipelineConfig spreads it into ANY config whose matchingConfig omits the key. A hand-created UHO/BCBS config that forgets startDate: null silently drops every row effective before July 2025 — counted in stats.skippedBeforeStartDate but easy to miss on a first onboarding run, and there is no boundary warning for the inheritance.

**Workaround today:** Seed every new carrier config with explicit matchingConfig.startDate: null and verify stats.skippedBeforeStartDate === 0 on the first run

#### Wizard hardcodes the first carrierConfig record — a second carrier cannot be selected

*Lens: parse-transform — `/Users/matthew/crm/packages/twenty-front/src/modules/reconciliation/hooks/useOpenReconciliationWizard.ts:63,77`*

openReconciliationWizard always calls openWithConfig(records[0], …) from an unordered useFindManyRecords list. There is no carrier picker and no caller passes a specific config (sole caller is useCreateNewIndexRecord.ts:44). Create a UHO carrierConfig record today and the import dialog will still open with whichever record happens to sort first — every run, every prefill write-back (including the columnMapping save at useOpenReconciliationImportDialog.ts:330-339) targets that record. This single line defeats the entire 'second carrier is pure config' achievement of the pipeline.

**Workaround today:** None safe in-product. Could mutate record order so the desired config is first, but the write-back then corrupts whichever config is selected; effectively single-carrier until a picker exists.

#### Never-seeded carrier silently runs as Ambetter with blanket statuses — required-role validation checks resolution, not presence, and the required set is a hardcoded global

*Lens: parse-transform — `/Users/matthew/crm/packages/twenty-server/src/modules/reconciliation/parsers/transforms.ts:670-673,710-722; /Users/matthew/crm/packages/twenty-server/src/modules/reconciliation/types/carrier-config.ts:297-304`*

Three compounding holes. (1) A carrierConfig with no statusConfig.engineId falls back to 'ambetter-bob-v1' with only a warning. (2) validateStatusRoleMapping iterates Object.entries(resolvedMapping) — an EMPTY fieldMapping (the natural state of a hand-created UHO config) yields zero unresolvedRequired and the run proceeds; per the file's own comment (transforms.ts:670-674) null effectiveDate blanket-defaults every row to ACTIVE_APPROVED and null paidThroughDate blanket-derives PAYMENT_ERROR_*. The fail-fast shipped for unresolved configured roles (parse.job.ts:144-173) never fires because nothing is configured. (3) REQUIRED_STATUS_ENGINE_ROLES is a module constant encoding ambetter-bob-v1 semantics — a future engine with different required inputs (e.g. lastPaymentDate) cannot declare its own set.

**Workaround today:** Operator must hand-seed a complete, correct statusConfig JSON before the first run and notice the deprecation warnings in server logs; nothing in-product stops a premature run.

#### Operator workflow: no carrier picker, no carrierConfig creation path — an engineer is required at steps 2, 5, and 6

*Lens: domain-ui — `packages/twenty-front/src/modules/reconciliation/hooks/useOpenReconciliationWizard.ts:63,77; packages/twenty-server/src/database/commands/custom/seed-ambetter-carrier-config.command.ts:374-377`*

To add UHO today: (1) create the carrier record — UI, fine; (2) create the carrierConfig — NO UI flow and the only seed command is Ambetter-specific, so either an engineer clones seed-ambetter-carrier-config.command.ts (deploy) or an admin hand-authors RAW_JSON (statusConfig with engineId + role fieldMapping against UHO's exact headers, transformRules, policyNumberPattern, productMapping with raw UUIDs) with zero save-time validation — errors surface only when the first run fails at PARSE (parse.job.ts:110-124) or MATCH; (3) carrierId must be linked or matching hard-fails (data.service.ts:119-126); (4) columnMapping is captured by the import dialog on first run — this part works; (5) BUT the wizard opens records[0] of an unordered carrierConfig find-many — with two configs, which carrier an import targets is arbitrary; there is no selection UI at all. Step 5 is the first thing that visibly breaks the moment a second config record exists, even for a perfectly configured carrier.

**Workaround today:** Keep only one carrierConfig record active at a time (delete/recreate between carriers), or engineer-drive: clone the seed command and patch the wizard ordering

#### Default startDate cutoff is Ambetter business history that every new carrier silently inherits

*Lens: domain-ui — `packages/twenty-server/src/modules/reconciliation/engines/matching.ts:315 (DEFAULT_MATCHING_CONFIG.startDate = '2025-07-09')`*

The code's own comment says it: 'an Omnia business-history constant, not a sensible cutoff for any other carrier.' But the defaults-merge in parseCarrierPipelineConfig means a new carrierConfig that simply omits matchingConfig.startDate gets Ambetter's onboarding date and silently drops every BOB row with an earlier effective date (match.job.ts:183-186). It is counted in stats.skippedBeforeStartDate, but nothing forces a new carrier's author to make an explicit choice — the Oscar e2e fixture has to explicitly set startDate: null and carries a warning comment (pipeline-real-data.spec.ts:859-861), proving operators must just know this.

**Workaround today:** Always seed matchingConfig.startDate explicitly (null or the carrier's real onboarding date) in every new config

#### Run wizard hardcodes the first carrierConfig record — no carrier selection

*Lens: operability — `packages/twenty-front/src/modules/reconciliation/hooks/useOpenReconciliationWizard.ts:53-80`*

openReconciliationWizard always calls openWithConfig(records[0]) (and latestRecords[0] in the retry path). The moment a UHO or BCBS carrierConfig record exists, which carrier a run targets depends on unspecified record ordering; there is no picker. The backend is multi-carrier-ready (config resolved per reconciliation.carrierConfigId), but the only operator entry point cannot express a choice.

**Workaround today:** Create the reconciliation record by hand with the right carrierConfigId, attach the file, and call startReconciliationParsing via GraphQL — not an operator-grade flow.

### blocks some carriers (UHO/BCBS-class) (21)

#### Status-engine registry has one entry and engines are code-only; engine requirements are a global constant

*Lens: config-surface — `packages/twenty-server/src/modules/reconciliation/engines/status.ts:387-389; parsers/transforms.ts:670-673`*

STATUS_ENGINES = { 'ambetter-bob-v1': deriveAmbetterStatus }. Payment/termination semantics — calendar-month placement (status.ts:164-175), current-month payment-error (status.ts:177-186), eligibleForCommission-as-cancel (status.ts:252-267), cancel-previous-version (status.ts:188-203) — are all inside that one function; the only knob is placedThresholdDays. UHO/BCBS with different payment semantics (status-code column, no eligible-for-commission flag, paid-to-date vs paid-through) need a new engine written, registered, and deployed. Worse, REQUIRED_STATUS_ENGINE_ROLES (['effectiveDate','paidThroughDate']) is a module constant explicitly 'confirmed against deriveAmbetterStatus' but enforced for every engine at parse (parse.job.ts:161-172) — a future engine that doesn't need paidThroughDate would have valid configs fail the run.

**Workaround today:** If the new carrier's semantics are the same ACA effective/paid-through/term rules, reuse 'ambetter-bob-v1' by mapping its columns into the four fixed roles — exactly what the Oscar e2e does (engines/__tests__/pipeline-real-data.spec.ts:25-31)

#### Tier internals hardcoded: per-tier confidences, tier-6 weights, name-score weights, date-proximity bands, agent-name normalization

*Lens: config-surface — `packages/twenty-server/src/modules/reconciliation/engines/matching.ts:767,787,811,831,879,1028 (confidences); 843-873 (tier-6 weights); 382 (0.4/0.6 name weights); 402-420 (proximity bands); 322,338 (US company suffixes, min length)`*

Only tier 7 has configurable confidence bands. Tiers 2-6 and 8 return literals (98/95/85/90/cap-70/60), tier-6's weighted score is fixed 40/30/20/10, memberNameScore weights last name 0.6/first 0.4, and dateProximityScore bands are fixed. For BCBS-style member-ID-centric books where the 'policy number' (subscriber ID) is shared by a whole family, tier 5's 'single match = definitive identifier, 90' (comment at matching.ts:820-824 even states the rationale is Ambetter's per-member U-number) over-states confidence; the only per-carrier levers are disabling tiers and moving autoMatchThreshold, which shifts ALL tiers at once.

**Workaround today:** Disable risky tiers via enabledTiers and raise autoMatchThreshold so identifier-only matches land in NEEDS_REVIEW — coarse, and drags tier-2/3 down with them

#### Matching-role registry is a code constant and dedup semantics are fixed keep-newest-per-policy

*Lens: config-surface — `packages/twenty-server/src/modules/reconciliation/engines/matching.ts:57-70; jobs/match.job.ts:653-689`*

MATCHING_ROLE_BY_CRM_FIELD hardcodes which CRM dot-paths feed which MatchInput role, and MatchInput's roles are fixed. A carrier whose identifier lives on a different CRM field (memberId/subscriberId rather than policyNumber) or needs an extra corroboration signal (plan ID, member suffix) requires editing the const registry — config cannot add an entry. Compounding: dedupPendingByPolicyId always keeps the single newest-effective-date row per matched policy. BCBS-style member-level files (one row per covered member sharing a subscriber ID) would collapse to one arbitrary member's row — subscriber-row selection or aggregation is not expressible. Cross-carrier note: legacy match overrides with null carrierName apply to every carrier (services/review-item.service.ts:1335-1346), so Ambetter-era overrides could hijack a colliding identifier on carrier two.

**Workaround today:** Map the carrier's member ID onto crmField 'policyNumber' if that is how the CRM stores it (works for UHO-like layouts); pre-process member-level files to subscriber-level before upload

*Lens: config-surface — `packages/twenty-server/src/modules/reconciliation/engines/diff.ts:741 (agent.*), 749 (currency), 263-285 (Jan-1 ACA rollover), 36/504 (name thresholds 0.98/0.85), 299-306 (lead-identity field set)`*

The business goal explicitly flags possible AOR differences for UHO/BCBS: a carrier whose BOB reports the actual writing agent would want agent drift surfaced, and there is no knob. #### Renaming a carrierConfig silently orphans learned rules and match overrides

*Lens: operability — `packages/twenty-server/src/modules/reconciliation/jobs/match.job.ts:779-788; services/decision-rule.service.ts:43,127-144; services/review-item.service.ts:1321-1346`*

#### No config versioning, rollback, or reviewable audit trail; admin-only raw-JSON editing is the entire UX

*Lens: operability — `packages/twenty-front/src/modules/object-record/record-field/ui/meta-types/input/components/RawJsonFieldInput.tsx:20-27; seed-ambetter-carrier-config.command.ts:190-195; services/object-lockdown.service.ts:24-29,155-163`*

- **Per-carrier diffRules knobs for the shared guard chain** (domain-ui) — The proposal reads the guard chain correctly and its defaults genuinely preserve Ambetter behavior (logicChange=false is honest for Ambetter), but it misunderstands why two of the three guards exist. They are not per-carrier policy preferences — they guard pipeline capabilities that do not exist yet. (1) syncCurrencyFields: there is no dollars-to-micros normalization anywhere in the diff or apply path. CRM holds premium.amountMicros (raw micros, data.service.ts:184); BOB holds dollar strings; CURRENCY compares 'exact' (diff.ts:617) and apply coerces with a bare Number() (coerceFieldDiffValueForRecordUpdate). A carrier flipping this bit gets a false premium diff on essentially every row, and applying writes dollar amounts into amountMicros — a 1e6 data-corruption footgun. The guard comment itself says suppression holds 'until we settle the semantics'; that is modeling/normalization code work, not a JSON bit. (2) syncAgentFields: resolveCrmObjectType (diff.ts:190) only knows lead/policy, and buildUpdatesForTarget (review-item.service.ts:991+) only routes to policyUpdates/leadUpdates — an agent.npn diff would attempt to write an 'agent' composite onto the policy record, where agent is a relation. Real agent sync means relinking agentId via NPN lookup, i.e. the underlying logic change the proposal excludes. (3) suppressEffectiveDateRollover is the only knob that works as a pure toggle, but its payoff for UHO/BCBS is speculative (the Jan-1 carry-forward pattern it suppresses is benign for any carrier, and the uncovered isBackwardsEffectiveDateMove guard still suppresses related moves). Net: the plumbing is small, but as scoped it ships two dangerous dead knobs and one marginal one. The right version of this idea bundles the knobs with currency normalization and an agent-relink apply path — a medium effort that IS a logic change — so the proposal as written should not be done.
